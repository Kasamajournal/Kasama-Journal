Feature Article

DepEd Targets Improved Performance of 8 Million Students for 2025 PISA

July 31, 2024 – In a significant education reform update, the Department of Education (DepEd) has announced its plan to enhance the academic performance of over 8 million Filipino students in preparation for the 2025 Programme for International Student Assessment (PISA). During the event, Secretary of Finance and former Senator Sonny Angara emphasized the importance of long-term investments in education to uplift national competitiveness and global rankings. The PISA exam evaluates students worldwide in reading, mathematics, and science.

DepEd’s renewed focus includes teacher training, better curriculum alignment, improved assessment systems, and collaborative governance with local governments. The campaign is expected to create measurable impacts on student learning outcomes and raise the Philippines’ standing in international education metrics.

This initiative is in line with broader education reforms that aim to deliver quality, inclusive, and future-ready education for all Filipino learners.
